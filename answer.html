Who Done It?

SCROOGE

The Truth

Scrooge was hired by the FBI to kill SATAN following a number of criminal activities.
However, due to the fact that he is dyslexic and not very good at reading, he did not understand the person he was supposed to kill and thought it said SANTA not SATAN.
He has therefore killed Santa by mistake.

Key Evidence

  • When the police searched him, they found an FBI name badge, a pen, a dictionary, and a used, empty envelope.
  • He has no qualifications, not even in English.
  • He claimed to be watching a movie and putting up his Christmas tree at the time of the murder, but this was his alibi.
  • The crumpled note reading “SATAN” found at the crime scene was actually meant for him—it was the target he was supposed to eliminate.
  • His dyslexia caused him to misread the name, leading to the tragic mistake.

Conclusion

This was a case of mistaken identity caused by a reading error. Scrooge, an FBI hitman, was supposed to eliminate “SATAN” but misread the name as “SANTA” due to his dyslexia.
The empty envelope found on him likely contained the original instructions from the FBI.
The dictionary was probably his attempt to verify the name, but his reading difficulties led to the fatal error.
